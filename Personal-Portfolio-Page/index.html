About Me

My name is Terry Murphy and I am a Chemical Engineer that has worked in the inudstrial automation space for many years. In my current position, I am responsible for the Process Control Systems that control two chemical plants in Sydney, Australia. This encompasses system hardware and software accountabilities and provides me with the opportunity to get paid to do something that I thoroughly enjoy.

I have ambitions to continue to apply the automation skills and knowledge I have acquired to every aspect of human endeavour where automation is being applied, without limiting my opportunities to the industrial space. I have particular interest in the application of Machine Learning & Artificial Intelligence and would one day love to end up contributing significantly to projects associated with driverless cars, general business automation, medical diagnostic improvements, data mining, to name but a few.

With this vision in mind, I realised that my skill set needed to expand to allow me to prepare for this next stage of my working life. And this is where Free Code Camp (FCC) and its founder, Quincy Larson, come to the fore, by providing this brilliant online environment to allow someone like me (anyone in fact), to equip themselves with the skills and knowledge to get them more than started on their way to becoming a Full Stack Developer.

And so this is how and why I can be in this position now, creating my own personal portfolio page as part of the FCC's Responsive Web Design module. I have worked very hard at this since starting and am getting better at this the more I spend on it (funny that!), but acknowledge that I am still for the most part clueless at the current time. Having said that, I am very proud of what I have achieved so far and am pretty stoked that I have created this page using plain old HTML and CSS, without any frameworks (Bootstrap, jQuery etc.) - this is something that I wanted to do while learning the fundamentals of this craft (ie. not become dependent on a framework).

I have learned so much along the way thus far in my journey. I am not rushing at all. Feel free to check out some of my responsive web design projects that I have completed below or by clicking on the Work link in the Navigation Bar at the top of this page.
